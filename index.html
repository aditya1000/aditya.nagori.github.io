About

I have a deep passion for biomedical data science and computational medicine. I earned my Ph.D. in computational medicine, where my thesis focused on predicting critical events in critically ill and asthmatic children. My research involved applying machine learning and statistical models to anticipate and mitigate disease progression.

Currently, I am a Data Scientist and Postdoctoral Fellow at the Duke University School of Medicine. I work on AI-driven healthcare projects, focusing particularly on the use of large language models for perioperative care.

I welcome collaboration with others passionate about biomedical data science. Together, we can explore data to advance our understanding and contribute to the improvement of human health.
